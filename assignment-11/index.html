Co-parenting works best when there is a high level of cooperation and low level of conflict between parents; educational programs help divorced parents learn communication techniques and separate issues between parents from raising their kids the best they can (Ferraro et al., 2016). Many couples choose to have a divorce mediator; not only can they help with general issues and legal matters, but many can walk you through a parenting plan. Having a consistent schedule is very important for children, especially with something as life-changing as this. Parenting plans include weekly schedules, who get the kids what days, which is responsible for what activities and contingency plans; holidays and special occasions are also decided upon this time.
If civility and healthy communication can occur, co-parents should still share time with the family; birthdays, school performances, and extracurriculars. However, if the adults cannot remain civil and be good role models for the children, it may be best to have some separation directly after divorce. The process of divorce is difficult for all members of the family. It is essential to go into the process openminded with humility and forgiveness. Remind your children that it is never their fault, and you both will continue to love them unconditionally.
